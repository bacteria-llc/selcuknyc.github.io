iPhone's front camera kills your look, right? Well, it just mirrors the photo (unlike Instagram and Snapchat cams) and you get your brain dead moment. Because you got so much used to your mirror face and your narcissistic ass can't accept that's you:)

An article online gave me the insight:

"We are used to identifying with our faces as they would appear in a mirror, but when we take a selfie, the camera captures our faces as strangers would see us from head on rather than we would see ourselves in a reflection. Emotionally unprepared to see our unsymmetrical faces tilting in a slightly different way, we sometimes approach selfies with confusion and disdain."

--

"While some say selfies make people narcissistic, others say that they actually make them more comfortable in their own skin. Because the more times you take a selfie, the more comfortable you are seeing your face in all angles."

When you get in a long relationship, you indeed see your partner from so many different angles eventually and you get used to that face you love. But there comes a time for an effect similar to the mirrored photo effect. You -really- see that very specific disturbing look of her face that annoys you to the core. Knowing that it's my faulty eyes and not her beautiful face can improve me as a person. Here I dive deep into the issue.
