Post 1

One of the more common OpenGL programming problems that I see concerns the poor precision of the Z buffer.

Many of the early 3D adaptors for the PC have a 16 bit Z buffer, some others have 24 bits - and the very best have 32 bits. If you are lucky enough to have a 32 bit Z buffer, then Z-precision may not seem to be an issue for you. However, if you expect your program to be portable, you'd better give it some thought. The Mirrored Selfie

iPhone's front camera kills your look, right? Well, it just mirrors the photo (unlike instagram and snapchat cam) and you get your brain dead moment. Because you got so much used to your mirror face and your narcissitic ass can't accept that's you:)

"We are used to identifying with our faces as they would appear in a mirror, but when we take a selfie, the camera captures our faces as strangers would see us from head on rather than we would see ourselves in a reflection. Emotionally unprepared to see our unsymmetrical faces tilting in a slightly different way, we sometimes approach selfies with confusion and disdain."

--

"While some say selfies make people narcissistic, others say that they actually make them more comfortable in their own skin. Because the more times you take a selfie, the more comfortable you are seeing your face in all angles."

When you get married, you indeed see your partner from soo many different angles eventually and you get used to that face you love. But there comes a time for an effect similar to the mirrored photo effect.
